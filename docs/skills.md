# Skills

A **skill** is a domain of practice that you excel at due to experience, training, or natural ability.

* Each character starts with proficiency in 5 skills.
* Expertise or proficiency in more skills can be gained through [class](classes.md) feats.

**Proficiency:** When you make a check that falls within the bounds of a skill you are proficient in, you gain +2 to the roll. Proficiency only applies to skill checks; it does not apply to weapons, attacks, or saves.

**Expertise:** Expertise is a higher level of proficiency. If you have expertise in a skill, you add +5 to the roll instead of +2.

**Ability:** A skill can be used with a variety of different ability scores, depending on the task.  The most commonly used abilities are given in examples for each skill below, but this can vary with the context.

**Overlap:** A given task might fall under more than one skill.  For example, safely jumping down from a moderate height might be Athletics or Acrobatics. Mountaineering might be Athletics or Survival, while climbing a drainpipe to a second-story window might be Athletics or Thievery. You can gain the benefits of being skilled if you have either one the applicable skills. However, you gain only a single bonus, even if you have multiple skills or a background that might apply to the roll.

## Skill List

The following list of 18 skills provides the most common uses and related ability scores for each.  These are only examples and do not form an exhaustive list of uses.

### Acrobatics

_Quick, lithe, or graceful full-body movements_

* **balancing** (DEX) - staying upright on narrow, slippery, uneven, or unsteady surfaces.
* **escaping** (DEX) - wriggling free of bonds, grapples, or through tight spaces; contorting.
* **tumbling** (DEX) - dives, rolls, or somersaults; dodging past foes.

### Animal Handling

_Working with beasts of animal-like intelligence_

* **empathy** (WIS) - discerning an animal's mood or intentions.
* **handling** (WIS or CHA) - calming or directing a domesticated animal; keeping a wild animal calm or redirecting an agitated one.
* **mimicry** (WIS or CHA) - imitating animal sounds.
* **riding** - directing a mount in a tricky maneuver (WIS or CHA); controlling an spooked mount (WIS, CHA, or STR).
* **training** (WIS or CHA) - rearing or training an animal.

### Arcana

_Lore concerning magic, other planes, and the occult._

* **artifice** (INT) - construction of magical items; legendary artifacts, sentient items; constructs.
* **magic** (INT) - magical traditions; magical theory; spellcraft, arcane rituals or ceremonial magic; runes, symbols, and glyphs.
* **planes** (INT) - other planes of existence, their natures, and native inhabitants; portals and gates.
* **occult** (INT) - esoteric and mystic philosophies; forbidden texts; the Far Realms and its denizens.

### Athletics

_Track-and-field activities_

* **climbing** (STR or DEX) - scaling or clinging to steep surfaces.
* **grappling** - wrestling and grabbing hold (STR) or slipping free of such holds (STR or DEX).
* **jumping** (STR or DEX) - leaping across a gap, over an obstacle, or up to reach something overhead.
* **running** - sprinting (DEX); chasing or fleeing (DEX or CON).
* **swimming** - keeping your head above rough water; swimming quickly; diving or submerging (STR or DEX); making headway through swift or rough water (STR or CON).

### Diplomacy

_Convincing others through tact, logic, or negotiation._

* **debate** (INT or CHA) - convincing through logical or factual argument.
* **gossip** - gathering information and rumors (WIS or CHA); sowing rumors or putting out the word (CHA).
* **negotiation** (CHA or WIS) - reaching a mutually beneficial agreement or compromise; bartering or haggling.
* **persuasion** (CHA) - convincing through eloquence, charm, or flattery.

### Fast-Talk

_Verbally tricking someone by hurrying them along before they have time to think._

* **blather** (CHA) - distracting or holding another's attention through outlandish antics.
* **deception** (CHA) - bluffing, lying, or conning.
* **taunt** (CHA) - slinging insults or otherwise maddening a foe into charging or attacking you.

### History

_Lore concerning the structure, practices, and artifacts of past and present societies_

* **civilizations** (INT) - history of the common (player) humanoid races, including architecture, art, etiquette, famous people, governance, heraldry, major events, royal lines, wars, etc.
* **exotic societies** (INT) - history of the more esoteric and savage humanoid races as well other intelligent races, such as fey or giants.
* **local** (INT) - history of all major events within a given area.

### Insight

_Your sensitivity to vibes, subtle connections, or unusual behavior._

* **investigation** (INT or WIS) - recalling, recognizing, or connecting important clues to form a clearer picture; detective work.
* **sense motive** (WIS or CHA) - reading the mood of a group or crowd; discerning whether a person is acting strangely or suppressing a strong emotion, such as nervousness, distraction, acting under duress, possessed, paying you undue attention, etc.
* **streetwise** (WIS) - recognizing the sort of person to ask about illicit goods or activities; finding the right part of town without needing to ask around.

### Intimidation

_Compelling others through your dominating presence or force of will._

* **command** (CHA) - issuing commands or directions to your underlings, especially when their morale is wavering.
* **interrogate** (CHA or WIS) - effectively questioning a prisoner.
* **intimidate** - threatening, scaring, or demoralizing someone (CHA); coercing them based a threat of you doing physical violence to them (STR).

### Medicine

_Anatomy and medical care._

* **diagnosis** (WIS or INT) - identifying a poison or disease; examining a body to determine a cause of death or the likely cause of visible wounds.
* **heal** (WIS or INT) - stabilizing a dying companion; treating a poison, disease, or infection; administering long-term care for wounds or injuries.

### Nature

_Lore concerning plants, animals, geography, and the natural world._

* **celestial** (INT) - stars, planets, astronomy, astrology, celestial navigation.
* **fauna** (INT) - natural animals and beasts; recalling their features, abilities, and weaknesses.
* **flora** (INT) - plants and plant-like creatures; recalling their features, effects, and weaknesses.
* **geography** (INT) - terrain, climate, ecosystems, weather, natural cycles; maps and cartography.

In more technically advanced settings, this skill approximates Natural Science.

### Perception

_Acute awareness of your surroundings, attention to fine detail, and danger sense._

* **appraise** (WIS or INT) - examining an item for flaws, signs of tampering or forgery, or to determine its likely value.
* **listen** (WIS) - keen hearing, whether listening at a closed door or noticing soft footsteps approaching from behind you.
* **search** (WIS or INT) - careful examination, such as when searching for secret doors, undetected traps, or hidden compartments.
* **spot** (WIS) - seeing hidden dangers, such as a stalking predator or the glint of a metal tripwire.

### Performance

_Entertaining audiences._

* **acting** (CHA) - includes costume and makeup (disguises) and impersonation (of a specific person or a general type of person).
* **motion** (CHA or DEX) - includes dancing, juggling, and miming.
* **music** (CHA) - includes singing or playing a musical instrument.
* **oration** (CHA) - includes singing, poetry, storytelling, riddles, and jokes.

### Religion

_Lore about faiths, deities, and their workings._

* **ceremony** (INT or CHA) - conducting the important ceremonies or rituals of your own faith.
* **death** (INT) - nature of the soul, the afterlife, lingering spirits, and the undead.
* **divinity** (INT) - deities, pantheons, mythic history, divine minions and servants (including fiends and celestials), and their homes (outer planes).
* **faiths** (INT) - key tenets, practices, symbols, beliefs, and rites of different religions.

### Stealth

_Sneaking and hiding._

* **hide** (DEX) - ducking out of sight; concealing yourself in shadows or foliage; camouflage.
* **move silently** - padding quietly over the ground; easing a door open without a squeak.
* **shadow** (DEX or CHA) - tailing someone in an urban environment; blending into a crowd.

### Survival

_Surviving in the wilderness._

* **camping** (WIS) - finding caves, building lean-tos; fire-starting; crafting basic camp tools from sticks and stones; rope use.
* **foraging** (WIS) - hunting, fishing, finding edible roots and berries, identifying clean drinking water, setting traps and snares.
* **tracking** (WIS) - following tracks; recognizing the spoor of dangerous beasts.
* **wayfinding** (WIS) - intuiting direction, weather sense, navigating, mountaineering; identifying and avoiding natural hazards.

### Technology

_Engineering and machinery._

* **architecture** (INT) - determining weak points or construction techniques in mines, buildings or fortifications; siege engines, demolition.
* **black powder** (INT) - aiming or maintaining bombards, cannons, or firearms (if present in the setting).
* **operations** (INT) - using complex tools or machinery; deducing the operation of a strange device or control panel.
* **vehicles** (INT or WIS) - fixing, maintaining, and operating vehicles, such as wagons, chariots, boats, or sailing ships.

This skill is more useful in settings with advanced technology, such as gunpowder, steampunk, weird science, spacefaring ships, etc.

### Thievery

_Trespassing and stealing._

* **burglary** - scaling a drainpipe to a second story window (STR or DEX); fencing stolen goods (WIS or CHA).
* **open lock** (DEX or INT) - picking locks.
* **sleight of hand** (DEX) - picking pockets; palming small objects, hiding small items on your person.
* **traps** (INT or DEX) - deducing the mechanics of a trap; disabling or reenabling traps; devising your own traps.

Despite the name, some non-thieves--such as assassins, spies, or undercover agents--make regular use of this skill.
