# Adventuring

## Damage

### Hit Points

Damage that you take reduces your current hit points (HP). When you reach 0 HP, you fall unconscious. Each point of remaining or future damage causes 1 wound.

Monsters & NPCs typically just die at 0 HP.

### Wounds

Wounds represent significant bodily injury. Track wounds by placing a slash or X mark on one of your equipment slot numbers. These ticks have no effect on the slot or item in it, though.

When all your slots are marked with wounds, you die.

#### Wound Penalty

If you have at least one wound, you suffer a penalty equal to (wounds - CON)(min: 0) to all your d20 rolls.

* When this wound penalty reaches -5, you are also reduced to half speed.
* When it reaches -10, you can no longer stand or walk unaided.

> **Tip:** To easily track your wound penalty (if your CON is not negative): Check off your initial wounds, up to CON, from the bottom of your available equipment slots. Then tick off any additional wounds from the top of the numbered slot list to easily see your current wound penalty.

### First Aid

If you suffered one or more wounds within the past hour, a character can spend a scene to make a WIS or INT medicine roll to treat your injuries. You make this roll at disadvantage if you are treating yourself. On a success, you heal 1 wound. A natural 1 causes a wound instead. Once a first aid attempt has been made, no further attempts can benefit you until you receive another wound.

### Direct Damage

Direct damage bypasses your HP and directly causes wounds. Monsters & NPCs instead take double damage to their HP from direct damage. Direct damage still counts as damage for effects that let you reduce or prevent incoming damage.

### Immunity, Resistance, and Vulnerability

* Some creatures are immune to certain kinds of damage. Damage of that type is reduced to 0 for them.
* If a creature is resistant to a type of damage, they take half the amount (rounded down, min 1).
* If they are vulnerable, they take double damage.

### Rests & Recovery

A short rest (usually an hour) lets you expend HD to recover HP. You also recover 1 expended HD, which you may immediately expend for HP if you wish. Cumulative short rests usually have no additional effect.

A long rest (usually a night of sleep in a safe haven) lets you recover all your HP and all your expended HD. You also heal 1 wound.
