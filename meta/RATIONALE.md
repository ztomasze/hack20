# Rationale

_This is my edition of D&D._

There are so many great games out there! Yet I always come back to Dungeons & Dragons. I dabbled in AD&D 2nd edition in college, I really got into D&D with 3rd edition, and I’ve loved 5th edition. I've also sampled Basic and 4th edition, and I still flip through my 1st edition books from time to time.  Even within that canon, each edition is mechanically quite different.

With the d20 OGL, the "D&D" family expanded: Pathfinder, the retroclones, the OSR movement, Shadowdark, and so many more. Too many to play them all, but each with interesting ideas and little twists to try.

In exploring that space, I have tried many different homebrew systems with the goal of producing a fantasy ttRPG that is not D&D… but each of them eventually drifts back to that core that I know so well.

Well, with this effort, I'm embracing that I'm building a "fantasy heartbreaker".  It's nothing new versus all the other d20 fantasy games out there, but this one is mine.

## Why not just play D&D or another d20 game?

5e comes closest to my favorite D&D flavor, but there's still things I'd like to tweak. Yet the more house rules I add, the harder it is for me and my players to remember what's still standard and what has been modified. The more sweeping the change, the more discordant and "breaking" that experience.

While every other d20 game has interesting things to try, none of them has cleanly fit what I want to play at my table.
